What the research says

Three findings stand out from the academic literature:

Interruptions are expensive. A single interruption costs ~23 minutes of recovery time before returning to deep focus. Gloria Mark, UC Irvine (2008)

Back-to-back meetings degrade quality. EEG measurements showed a ~40% drop in attention and engagement in the second consecutive meeting. Microsoft Human Factors Lab (2021)

Meetings spawn more meetings. About 30% of meetings generate informal follow-up discussions that aren't on anyone's calendar. HBR, "Stop the Meeting Madness" (2019)

Isolated, these findings are interesting. Combined, they describe a system where time leaks out in ways no one measures — and no one is accountable for.

Signs your meetings need attention

Standups have crept past 18 minutes

Scrum Guide recommends 15 minutes. When they drift longer, it's usually a sign that problem-solving has replaced status updates.

Back-to-back meeting blocks

No buffer between meetings means each one starts with a mental context-switch penalty.

Recurring meetings no one can explain

"We've always had this Wednesday sync." UnMeet detects meetings that haven't changed in months.

People working around the meetings

When your team's real work happens before 9am or after 6pm, the calendar is the problem.
